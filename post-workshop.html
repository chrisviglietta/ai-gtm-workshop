The build · Real-world workflow

CRM enrichment loop

Maya came in with 2,400 stale HubSpot accounts — companies that signed up, never converted, and went silent. Sales had written them off; she suspected the data had.

She built one Clay table. Inputs: the dead-account list. Each row gets refreshed firmographics from a 3-source waterfall (Clearbit, ZoomInfo, Apollo), funding signals from the last 90 days, and a Claygent column scoring fit against Linear's ICP. Anything scoring 7+ writes back to HubSpot with a fresh "warm" tag and drops a Slack ping into #gtm-revival.

Two days after demo day it found 47 accounts that had quietly raised Series B in Q1. Three were already in Linear's funnel under the wrong owner.

Loom · Maya's live build · 4 min 12 s
Workflow spec
Inputs
2,400 dormant HubSpot accounts (CSV export from "Closed-Lost · 2024" view) plus a Salesforce ICP definition pasted as a Notion link.
Outputs
Refreshed firmographics, recent funding events, ICP-fit score (0–10), Claygent's plain-English reasoning per row.
Target system
HubSpot (write-back via native integration), Slack #gtm-revival (digest message), CSV download for the SDR team.
Tools used
Clearbit ZoomInfo Apollo Claygent HubSpot Slack

What this workshop required

Pre-flight, then liftoff

The Clay AI GTM Workshop is a half-day intensive — a four-hour live build, IRL or virtual, with Clay engineers in the room. Three pre-flight checks gate the seat — about fifteen minutes of work in total. The workshop itself is graded on what gets shipped.

Step 01 · Optional

Fuel up

What Maya did Watched the Intro to Clay crash course (~3 min).
  • One short video. Tables, sources, enrichments, AI columns — the vocabulary the room will use all day.
  • Optional, but every graduate who's skipped it has regretted it by lunch.
  • Sets the floor so we can spend the four hours building, not defining terms.
Step 02 · Required

Brainstorming

What Maya did Answered all 5 prompts. Showed up with the workflow already half-thought-through.
  • Operator role and ICP — who you sell to, in one line.
  • The workflow you want to build, in plain English.
  • Current stack and where Clay has to plug in (Salesforce → HubSpot + Slack, in Maya's case).
  • The biggest pain — the thing that'll still hurt on Monday if we don't fix it.
Step 03 · Required

Comms check

What Maya did Posted in #clay-workshops, auto-drafted from her brainstorming answers. 11 reactions before workshop day.
  • Public-by-default. The community sees what you're building before you walk in.
  • The post writes itself from Step 02 — role, ICP, workflow, stack, pain.
  • The reactions become the room you walk into on workshop day.
Three checks. About fifteen minutes. Workshops are graded on what gets shipped, not what gets attempted — and the pre-flight is the thing that makes shipping in four hours possible.
